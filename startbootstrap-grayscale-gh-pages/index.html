Why Wolves?

Apart from being the coolest animal, wolves carry a deep emotional connection for me. When I was very young, around three or four, I had an uncle who would often call my dad to talk and near the end of their conversation would ask my dad to put me on the phone. When the phone was handed to me, he would always ask me to howl like a wolf, which of course being that young I would always do. My dad would ask my uncle why he would do that everytime they talked to which he would reply, "I bet you he'll always remember me." It's crazy to think that something so small could shape somebody's life and leave lasting impacts decades later. Rest easy Uncle Don.

The Grey Wolf

Currently there are only two species of wolf in the world (those being the grey wolf and red wolf). My favorite of the two is undisputedly the grey wolf.

Timber Wolves

There are many grey wolf subspecies but the timber wolf is the best out of them all. Timber wolves are grey wolves commonly found in the forest, and usually range in color, although black/dark grey is my favorite.

Artic Wolves

Another great grey wolf subspecies is the artic wolf. Artic wolves are usually found in the artic (of course), and their pure white fur gives them a majestic quality that stands out from other wolves.
